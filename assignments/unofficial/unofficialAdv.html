Valuable Advice from People Within Industry

Coding Rocks!

Documentation:

"Documentation is the best way to remember things you did a year ago." It is easy to look up old code and see how you did things however,
it may not be clear why you did it a certain way. There are so many important decisions to make in order to problem-solve your own code as
you write it, that you will probably not be able to remember the significance behind it. Being able to write your code in prose will not only
reinforce your own understanding, but may very well provide you with a reason to pat your own back later on!
- Ms. Barbara Liau

Vaulnerability:

"Bring it down, then raise it up!" This was in response to a question I asked about being the only female in a male-dominated feild.
These words were a response to a contexted in which I had expressed my frustration at feeling like the "weak link." The gentlemen I
worked with had to reinforce all information I relayed it to them, but never questioned anything they said to one
another in the same mannor. Her advice was to be vaulnerable; open a channel of communication that irradicates competitive vibes, or
unecessary expectations of one another. Facilitate a space in which everyone can be genuine, human, and "self-aware". "Then, raise it."
This means "set the standard," or be the change you want to see in the room. If you want to be respected, you need to be taken seriously,
this means being the bigger person, working with your heart and NOT your ego.

Be Unique and True to YOU:

Code reflects who you are! If you aren't confident, your code has greater potential to be faulty.
